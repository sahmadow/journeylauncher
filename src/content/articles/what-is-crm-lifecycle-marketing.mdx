Most businesses treat CRM as a contact database. They store emails, log calls, and maybe send a monthly newsletter. That's not lifecycle marketing — that's a spreadsheet with extra steps.

**CRM lifecycle marketing** is the practice of designing automated, personalised communication flows that guide customers through every stage of their journey — from first touch to loyal advocate.

## The Lifecycle Stages

Every customer moves through predictable stages:

1. **Awareness** — They discover your brand
2. **Consideration** — They evaluate your offering
3. **Conversion** — They make a purchase or sign up
4. **Onboarding** — They learn to use your product
5. **Retention** — They stay engaged and come back
6. **Advocacy** — They refer others

The gap between "having a CRM" and "doing lifecycle marketing" is automation. Each stage should trigger the right message, at the right time, through the right channel.

## Why Most Companies Get It Wrong

Three common mistakes:

### 1. Batch-and-blast emails
Sending the same newsletter to your entire list ignores where each contact is in their journey. A new lead doesn't need the same message as a 2-year customer.

### 2. No segmentation
Without proper segments, personalisation is impossible. At minimum, segment by lifecycle stage, engagement level, and purchase history.

### 3. Manual processes
If your team is manually sending follow-ups, you're leaving revenue on the table. Automation handles the repetitive work so your team can focus on strategy.

## Building Your First Lifecycle Flow

Start simple. Pick one lifecycle gap — the biggest drop-off point in your customer journey — and build an automated flow for it.

For most businesses, that's **post-purchase onboarding**:

- **Day 0:** Welcome email with quick-start guide
- **Day 2:** Tips for getting the most value
- **Day 5:** Check-in — "How's it going?"
- **Day 14:** Feature highlight they haven't tried
- **Day 30:** Feedback request + referral prompt

This single flow can dramatically improve retention without any manual effort.

## What Tools Do You Need?

You don't need an enterprise CRM to start. The essentials:

- **Email platform** with automation (Klaviyo, Customer.io, Brevo)
- **Segmentation** based on behaviour and lifecycle stage
- **Event tracking** to trigger flows automatically
- **Analytics** to measure what's working
