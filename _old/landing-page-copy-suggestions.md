# Landing Page Copy Revision v2

Integrates the "Lovable for distribution" narrative with the agentic terminal system. All copy uses "We."

---

## 1. Hero Label

**Current:** `AGENTIC MARKETING SYSTEMS`

**Suggested:** `FROM PRODUCT TO CUSTOMERS`

---

## 2. Hero Headline

**Current:**
> Deploy Complete Marketing Systems. Straight From the Terminal.

Three variants — all built from the Problem 01 insight that anyone can build a product now but almost nobody can market one:

**Variant A:**
> Anyone Can Build a Product Now. We Make Sure People Actually Buy It.

**Variant B:**
> Lovable Builds Your Product. We Build the System That Sells It.

**Variant C:**
> Anyone Can Build a Product Now. Almost Nobody Can Market One. We Fix That.

**Why:** "Instantly" was wrong — we're not instant. We custom-build an agentic system that then scales exponentially. These variants set up the real promise: we close the gap between building a product and getting customers, through a system that compounds over time.

---

## 3. Hero Subtitle

**Current:**
> We built a system where you vibe back and forth with Claude Code and MCPs to produce research, positioning, landing pages, and ads — the same way you'd vibe code any product. The result: a testing machine that ships campaigns at a speed agencies can't match.

**Suggested:**
> Lovable lets anyone build an app. We let anyone market it. You tell us about your business — AI agents handle the research, generate creative variants, configure targeting, and launch a live ad campaign.

**Why:** Instantly positions Vibe Marketing in the ecosystem the audience already knows. The Lovable reference is the sharpest framing. The shift from "terminal" to "AI agents handle" makes the value clear: the heavy lifting is fully automated.

---

## 4. Hero CTA

**Current:** `Book a Free Session`

**Suggested:** `Book Your Free 30-Min Session`

**Why:** Adding "30-Min" reduces perceived commitment.

## 5. Hero Categories (the row of labels beneath the headline)

**Suggested:** Research / Positioning / SEO / Lead Magnets / Ad Variants / Email Automations / Targeting / Reporting

**Why:** These map to the concrete deliverables of the system. Visitors immediately see the breadth of what gets built.

**Suggested:** `HERE'S WHAT HAPPENED`

## 7. Problem Section Heading

**Current:**
> You have AI tools. You still don't have a deployment pipeline.

**Suggested:**
> You're paying for months of agency work. We ship the same output in hours.

**Why:** Directly attacks the cost and time problem. Agencies and freelancers charge for time. This system leverages the best AI agents to compress a month of agency work into a few hours — with shorter time-to-market, greater flexibility, and rapid testing built in.

---

## 8. Problem Point 01

**Current:**
> **Prompting isn't a system.**
> You're copying and pasting between ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Canva. Nothing feeds into anything else. Every asset starts from scratch.

**Suggested:**
> **Anyone can build a product now. Almost nobody can market one.**
> Lovable, Bolt, Replit — they collapsed software development into a prompt. Millions of new products ship every year from people who never would have built anything five years ago. But they all hit the same wall: how do I get customers?

**Why:** Validates the visitor's situation. They're not bad at marketing — the tools just haven't caught up.

---

## 9. Problem Point 02

**Current:**
> **You can't test at scale.**
> One landing page. One ad. One version. By the time you manually build a second variant, the market has moved on. Speed is the moat — and you don't have it.

**Suggested:**
> **There's no "Lovable for distribution."**
> You can vibe-code a product in an afternoon. But marketing it still means learning ad platforms, writing copy from scratch, guessing at targeting, and hoping something sticks. The creation side got democratized. The distribution side stayed gatekept.

**Why:** Names the gap explicitly. "There's no Lovable for distribution" is the most memorable line — it should be on the page.

---

## 10. Problem Point 03

**Current:**
> **Your marketing doesn't compound.**
> Without a connected system, every campaign is a one-off. Research doesn't inform positioning. Positioning doesn't flow into assets. Nothing builds on itself.

**Suggested:**
> **Agencies won't touch you at this stage. DIY tools leave you on your own.**
> You don't have the budget for a full agency. And the DIY tools expect you to already know what you're doing — which dashboards to read, which metrics matter, what "good" looks like. You need someone in between.

**Why:** Positions Vibe Marketing as the middle ground — neither expensive agency nor abandoned self-serve.

**Current:** `THE SYSTEM`

**Suggested:** `WE'RE BUILDING IT`

## 12. Solution Section Heading

**Current:**
> Vibe your marketing into existence. Three layers. One terminal.

**Suggested:**
> Tell us about your business. We build you a marketing system that runs on AI agents.

**Why:** The point isn't the terminal — it's that AI agents do the heavy lifting. Research, positioning, creative, targeting — all automated, all AI-driven. You describe your business, we custom-build a system where agents handle the work that used to require a team.

---

## 13. Solution Point 01

**Current:**
> **Research — scrape competitors, find gaps.**
> Using Perplexity and Playwright MCPs, we pull live competitor data, customer reviews, and market positioning gaps. You start with intelligence, not assumptions.

**Suggested:**
> **AI agents extract your value proposition and research your market.**
> Using Perplexity and Playwright MCPs, agents scrape your site, analyze your competitors, pull customer reviews, and identify positioning gaps — automatically. You don't write a brief. The system generates one.

**Why:** Starts with what the visitor experiences ("we extract"), then names the tools for credibility.

---

## 14. Solution Point 02

**Current:**
> **Foundation — define positioning and brand voice.**
> Expert instruction manuals (Skills) encode Schwartz and Ogilvy frameworks directly into the AI. Your positioning, messaging angles, and voice guidelines are generated from your research — not from generic prompts.

**Suggested:**
> **We generate creative variants and configure targeting.**
> Using expert Skills built on Schwartz and Ogilvy frameworks, we produce multiple positioning angles, ad copy variants, and landing page versions — then configure targeting based on your actual market data. Not guesswork. Not templates.

**Why:** Aligns with the new flow (URL → variants → targeting). The frameworks are still here but serve the narrative rather than leading it.

---

## 15. Solution Point 03

**Current:**
> **Assets — deploy landing pages, ads, and lead magnets.**
> Programmatically create video ads via Remotion, build interactive lead magnets, and ship conversion-optimized landing pages — all from the terminal. Each asset inherits your positioning, so everything is consistent.

**Suggested:**
> **AI agents build your assets, set guardrails, and launch.**
> Video ads via Remotion. Static image variants via Glyph and Nano Banana Pro. Interactive lead magnets built programmatically. Conversion-optimized landing pages that avoid every AI design trope. Agents set intelligent budget guardrails and launch — all in one session.

**Why:** Completes the pipeline. The visitor now sees the full journey: URL → research → variants → targeting → budget → launch.

---

## 15b. Solution Point 04 (NEW — Email Automations)

**Current:** (does not exist on page)

**Suggested — add as a 4th solution row:**
> **AI agents spin up email automations in minutes, not weeks.**
> Full sequences — welcome flows, nurture campaigns, follow-ups — configured and ready to send. Test subject lines, timing, and offers at scale. What takes an agency weeks to scope and build, agents set up in a single session.

**Why:** Email is a missing piece in the current page. It's a core deliverable and a major differentiator — the speed of setup (minutes vs. weeks) and the ability to rapidly test variants at scale is the same "testing machine" advantage that applies to ads and landing pages.
